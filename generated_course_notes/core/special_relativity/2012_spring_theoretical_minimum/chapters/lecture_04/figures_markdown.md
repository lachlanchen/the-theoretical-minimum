## Diagram And Layout Reading
- `lecture_04_figure_02.png` preserves a very useful board layout: the upper right still contains the action, the center contains the definition of the Lagrangian, and the lower left begins the Euler–Lagrange operator. The leftover axis at the far left, with horizontal \(t\) and a lower \(b\), ties the formula to the earlier point-particle trajectory sketch.
- `lecture_04_figure_03.png` is organized as a vertical derivation. The top line is the field-theory action, the middle line is the familiar one-dimensional Euler–Lagrange equation, and the lower line begins the spacetime generalization. The frame matters because it shows the lecture’s logic spatially on the board: first recall the particle equation, then generalize it.
- `lecture_04_figure_04.png` is less of a board-writing frame and more of a speaking frame, but it still preserves a useful juxtaposition: the nonrelativistic \(L=T-V\) form remains central while Susskind is verbally interpreting the spatial-gradient terms as part of the potential side.
- `lecture_04_figure_05.png` is the cleanest structural board snapshot of the Euler–Lagrange generalization. It makes the analogy explicit by stacking the ordinary mechanics equation above the field-theory equation. If one wants the reader to see the shift from \(d/dt\) to \(\partial/\partial x^\mu\), this frame does that.
- `lecture_04_figure_06.png` is a two-level board layout. The upper board holds the interval relation and its squared form, while the lower board moves to a generic four-vector \(A^\mu\) and its Lorentzian norm. The visual bridge is important: metric first, then four-vector norm, then the claim that the same algebra produces invariants.

## TeX Reconstruction Plan

  A=\int_a^b dt\,L,
  \qquad
  L=\frac{m}{2}\dot\phi^2-V(\phi),
  \qquad
  \frac{d}{dt}\frac{\partial L}{\partial\dot\phi}=\frac{\partial L}{\partial\phi}.
  \]

The notes should reconstruct the mathematics explicitly in LaTeX nearby rather than relying on the screenshot.

  \[
  \frac{\partial}{\partial x^\mu}\left(\frac{\partial\mathcal L}{\partial(\partial_\mu\phi)}\right)-\frac{\partial\mathcal L}{\partial\phi}=0,

  \[
  \frac{\partial}{\partial x^\mu}\left(\frac{\partial\mathcal L}{\partial\left(\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial x^\mu}\right)}\right)-\frac{\partial\mathcal L}{\partial\phi}=0.
